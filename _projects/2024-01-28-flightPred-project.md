## What causes flight delays?

FAA estimates that delays to commercial aviation cost the airlines over $3 billion a
year and projects that delays throughout the system will continue to increase as the demand for passenger travel rises. Moreover, passengers are directly affected by the inconvenience of delays in terms of missed flight connections, missed business
meetings, and lost personal time.

Both the Bureau of Transportation Statistics (BTS) and FAA reported increases in
flight delays between 1995 and 1999.   Not only were there more delays in 1999
but the length of delays also increased.  The Inspector General,U.S. Department of Transportation found in a 2000 report  that most delays took place on the ground.  Some of those delays are:

* Taxi Out
* Taxi In
* Longer Flight Times
